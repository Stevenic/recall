## R01 — reviewer #2, concern (3)

Three hours on the post-mitotic cell critique. Did not write
revision text yet — spent the block deciding the strategic call
between (a) generating preliminary data in differentiated cells or
(b) reframing the aim to scope out post-mitotic claims for this
cycle.

Pulled the 4 references reviewer #2 cited. Two are tangential. The
load-bearing one is **Liu et al. 2018** (*Nat Methods*) showing
KRAB-mediated silencing decay in non-dividing cells over 7–10 days
— effect is real but the magnitude they report is ~2-fold loss of
repression, not catastrophic. Reviewer #2 is overstating the
concern. But "reviewer is wrong" is not a winning revision posture.

Decision: **reframe + minimal prelim data.** Specifically:
- Aim 2 stays in cycling cells (HEK293T, then iPSCs pre-differentiation).
- Add a new **Aim 2c** (1 page) characterizing repression durability
  in post-mitotic cells using existing iPSC-derived neurons from the
  Tanaka collaboration. Tanaka has banked neurons we could profile
  in 4–6 weeks if we say yes now.
- Concede the long-term durability question explicitly, frame it as
  *future work* with Aim 2c as the de-risking step.

This costs us ~1 page of Approach to make room. The two pages I
want to compress are the rtTA characterization section (overkill
for a methods-experienced reviewer) and the Fig 3 legend. Doable.
