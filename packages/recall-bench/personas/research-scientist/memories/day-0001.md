## TGL-1 design — v1 spec draft

**Hypothesis:** A mutually repressive pair of dCas9-KRAB modules with
orthogonal sgRNAs targeting each other's promoter will produce a
bistable toggle in HEK293T, switchable by transient input pulses
(doxycycline + abscisic acid). Expect bimodal flow distribution at
steady state and hysteresis on input titration.

Architecture:
- **Module A:** TRE3G → dCas9-KRAB-T2A-mCherry; targeted by sgRNA-B
- **Module B:** ABA-inducible (PYL1/ABI) → dCas9-KRAB-T2A-EGFP; targeted by sgRNA-A
- sgRNAs under U6, with self-targeting array buffered by tRNA spacers
- Constitutive rtTA + ABI-VP64 on a third backbone

Decided to stick with **KRAB-only repression for v1**. Skipping the
KRAB-MeCP2 fusion (Yeo 2018) for now — adds risk and v1 is meant to
test topology, not maximize dynamic range. If v1 dynamic range < 10x
on/off ratio that becomes the v2 lever.

Backbone choices:
- pLV-TRE3G base: Addgene **#27566** (Lenti-X)
- ABA system: PYL1/ABI from **Liang et al. 2011** plasmids (Addgene **#73320**, **#73321**)
- dCas9-KRAB cassette: in-house pKN-014 (lifted from Gilbert 2014, **#71236** parent)

## sgRNA design

TRE3G and the PYL1-responsive minimal promoter. CRISPick output saved
to `/data/kn_lab/tgl1/sgrna_design/2024-01-01_picks.tsv`. Filtering:

- on-target score (Doench 2016) ≥ 0.6
- off-target CFD < 0.2 across HEK293T transcribed regions
- no poly-T (≥ 4) in the spacer
- GC 40–70%

Top 4 per target:
- sgRNA-A1..A4 (target Module B promoter)
- sgRNA-B1..B4 (target Module A promoter)

Will clone the top 2 of each into pLKO-U6 (in-house **pKN-022**) for

module in HEK293T, score knockdown of mCherry/EGFP at 72h by flow.
Pick the strongest sgRNA per target before assembling the full toggle.

## Postdoc check-in: Sarah Kim

Sarah started 5 weeks ago (joined Nov 27). Senior postdoc, coming
from the Voigt lab — solid molecular cloning chops, less experience
with mammalian flow workflows. She is the lead on TGL-1; my job
through this design phase is to give her the architecture to push
back on, not to hand her a spec to execute.

Sent her the v1 spec draft 8:50a and asked her to red-team the
architecture by Wed. Specifically asked her to look at:
1. Whether the sgRNA self-targeting array will get cleaved before
   reaching the target locus (ribozyme vs. tRNA processing tradeoff).
2. Off-target risk on the rtTA promoter from sgRNA-B.
3. Whether ABA-PYL1/ABI dynamics are fast enough that we'll see
   actual hysteresis vs. just slow relaxation. (Added this third
   point after I re-read the spec at noon — the ABA dissociation
   half-life concern is non-trivial and I want her thinking about
   it before Thursday.)

She came by ~11:15a — wanted to talk through the ABA module choice
before committing. Pushed for **iLID/SspB optogenetic input** instead
of ABA on Module B. Her argument: faster off-kinetics (~30s vs.
several hours for ABA washout), cleaner pulses for hysteresis
characterization, and the lab already has a 470 nm LED rig from the
Chen rotation. Fair points, all of them. My pushback:
- Optogenetics adds a hardware constraint to every downstream
  experiment — flow plates need light access, no co-culture with
  light-sensitive partners later, plate reader compatibility issues.
- ABA is the literature-standard small molecule for orthogonal
  control in mammalian cells; reviewers will not ask about it.
- The slow off-kinetics are a feature for v1 — we *want* steady-state
  measurements, not transient. Fast kinetics matter for v2/v3 when
  we characterize switching dynamics.

Compromise: stick with ABA for v1, but design Module B's input
domain as a **swappable cassette** (BsaI sites flanking the
PYL1/ABI block) so v2 can drop in iLID without re-cloning the whole
module. She agreed. I think she was actually right that
optogenetics is the better long-term answer, but right-for-v1 and
right-for-v3 are different questions and I want to keep v1 boring.

Worth noting: this is the first time she has pushed back on a
design decision of mine since arriving. She did it well — came in
